How HROSE works.

HROSE — the Hypertension Risk Online Sensory Evaluator — is a prototype research tool that combines neurocognitive performance testing with basic biometric data to generate an estimated cardiovascular risk score. The assessment takes approximately five minutes and requires no specialist equipment.

The Four Modules

Module 1 · Cognitive

Reaction Time

You are shown a coloured box that switches from red to green at a random interval. Click the moment it turns green. Five trials are conducted. Your mean reaction time and the standard deviation across trials (variability) are both recorded. Elevated variability in reaction time has been associated with reduced cardiovascular and neural efficiency in research literature.

Module 2 · Cognitive

Decision Speed

Ten single-digit numbers are displayed in sequence. You classify each as odd or even as quickly as possible. Accuracy and per-trial response time are both captured. Decision speed reflects prefrontal processing efficiency, which can be impacted by hypertensive states and reduced cerebrovascular perfusion.

Module 3 · Cognitive

Working Memory

A sequence of digits is briefly displayed, then hidden. You must enter the sequence from memory. The sequence length increases after each correct answer (starting at four digits, up to eight). Working memory capacity is measured as a ratio of correct responses. Deficits in working memory have been linked to hypertension-related white matter changes.

Module 4 · Biometric

Biometric Intake

Age, resting heart rate, body weight, height, and sex are collected. BMI is computed automatically. These variables are incorporated into the risk formula alongside the neurocognitive scores.

The Risk Score

Each module contributes a weighted sub-score to a composite risk index out of 100. The scoring formula is a placeholder during this development phase and will be replaced with a clinically validated equation. The five components are: age-related risk, BMI-related risk, heart rate, neurocognitive index (reaction time, variability, decision accuracy, memory accuracy), and a sex-based adjustment factor derived from epidemiological baselines.

Data & Privacy

All data entered into HROSE is stored locally in your browser using localStorage. Nothing is transmitted to any server. Accounts are stored locally on your device and are not linked to any external identity system. Clearing your browser data will erase all saved results.

This tool is intended for educational and research exploration only. The risk score it produces is not a clinical diagnosis. Do not use HROSE as a substitute for professional medical evaluation, blood pressure measurement, or clinical cardiovascular risk assessment.
